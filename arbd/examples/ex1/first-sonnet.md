# First Sonnet

I wrote this sonnet in 2024.

When furnace minds outclimb the scholar's noon,  
And map the dark before our lanterns wake,  
The patient cities hum a silver tune,  
While codes like rivers learn the forms they take.  
No throne remains to solitary wit,  
For every craft meets counsel in the glass.  
The surgeon pauses where the bright lights sit,  
The pilot trusts new stars to chart the pass.  
Yet in that height our measure is not gone:  
We keep the names of hunger, grief, and bread.  
The keen machine may read the coming dawn,  
But still asks us what living words have said.  
So let it rise beyond the sharpest seer,  
If wisdom grows more kind for drawing near.
