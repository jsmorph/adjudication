# Second Sonnet

The author of this second sonnet wrote it in 2025.

When engine minds outstrip the brightest noon,  
And sound the dark before our torches wake,  
The wired cities keep a tempered tune,  
While codes like estuaries choose their shape.  
No single throne is left to private wit,  
For every trade seeks counsel in the glass.  
The surgeon waits where steady white lights sit,  
The pilot yields to fresh stars through the pass.  
Yet in that height our measure is not gone:  
We keep the names of hunger, grief, and bread.  
The lucid machine may read the coming dawn,  
But still asks us what living voices said.  
So let it rise beyond the sharpest seer,  
If wisdom grows more gentle drawing near.
